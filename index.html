1. Your Experience Will Be Everything:

Picture it: midnight is approaching and you gaze deep into the sky while on Earth. What is in your sight would likely be a moon with stars scattered around it. Now, remagine that exact scenerio, but on Neptune. What do you see? Likely not one, not two, not three, not four... but sixteen moons—sixteen times the merrier! Oh and take a glance (actually, more like a stare into your telescope) What do you see? If in the correct position, an exquisite, light blue-ish green white-ish gas icy gas giant; you've guessed it: Uranus! Those experiences are unique to the one and only Neptune. 2. Get the Most Out of Your Life!

Sick of Earth? You know, you wake up after who knows how long of being unconscious and paralyzed and you already have so many hours before it is the next time you get to be this way. You spend the next sixteen hours just waiting for that satisfying monment of deep rest. Well, on Neptune, days are two thirds the lemgth on Earth. (In other words, they are sixteen hours.) That means you get to spend half of your day resting up. That means you spend shorter intervals beetween being asleep and awake. So, when in doubt, switch your planet of habitat to Neptune to experience shorter, faster-paced cycles of day and night!

3. Surplus of a Valuable Gemstone

So, what is that precious, valuable gemstone that everyone would desire to have, but none (if any) of us have the ability to obtain, you ask? You’ve put your finger on its name: diamond! That mineral symbolizing eternal purity has its place on Earth in only a very few places. Due to the very specific, extreme geological conditions required for their formation, it’s no wonder that they’re often considered extraordinarily rare—that is, if you’re on planet Earth. But we’re talking Neptune here, and Neptune doesn’t just have an abundance of diamonds; it even rains diamonds… in an ongoing, continuous manner! So, not only can you collect your sacred gemstone like it’s candy, but you can also make a profit of thousands for each diamond. Easy money right there!

5. More to Explore

You’ve likely thought to yourself, “Earth is an incredibly large planet; I wish I could explore all of it!” Turns out, the Earth has a diameter of almost 8,000 miles. That, if you think about it, is not really that large, especially in comparison to other planets. That being said, take it or leave it, but Neptune has a diameter of slightly more than 30,000 miles—almost four times as much as that of Earth. Why is size so significant? The famous quote “Bigger is better” really applies here, because with that, imagine homes, cities, and all the buildings of wonder. They would have had incredibly more space, and with that space, who knows what could have been built?
